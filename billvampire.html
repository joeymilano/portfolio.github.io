1Commercial Design System

The update is not only a visual refresh. It reframes the product around an urgent job-to-be-done, a low-friction preview, a clear one-time price, and analytics that show where intent turns into payment.

AcquisitionProblem-first landing

Lead with the painful moment: surprise charge, trial renewal, or hard-to-cancel service.

ActivationResults before account

Let users paste, upload, or speak before signup so the product proves value early.

MonetizationOne-time rescue kit

Package payment around urgency: cancel path, refund script, evidence list, and reminder.

MeasurementEvent taxonomy

Instrument each decision point from landing view to kit purchase and saved case file.

2Background & Motivation

"A user who just got charged does not want a dashboard. They want the next action."

The original subscription tracker had personality, but the updated product needed sharper commercial intent. I moved the value proposition from broad spending awareness to a specific rescue workflow: detect the charge, choose the emergency, generate the kit, then unlock the assets worth paying for.

Urgent rescue positioning
No-bank-login trust strategy
Pay only for the complete kit
Luxury gothic visuals make the emergency feel serious, memorable, and monetizable.

5Design Challenges

  • Move from entertainment to urgency without losing the memorable Bill Vampire brand.
  • Earn trust without bank access by showing clear inputs, evidence handling, and practical next steps.
  • Create a $4.99 pay moment that feels like a fair unlock, not a surprise paywall.

1Background & Motivation

"Everyone's got stuff they don't need. Trashing it feels wasteful. What if giving it away was as effortless as scrolling?"
The pile at home: useless to you, painful to toss, hassle to give.
List it second-hand? Photo, price, haggle, ship—the thought alone kills it.
So it gathers dust. The stuff isn't the problem; giving it away is too heavy a lift.

WeNeed tries one thing: make giving away stuff as effortless as scrolling. No money—just We Points. Give to earn, claim to spend.

3Design Challenges

  • Scrolling has to feel good, but not so good you forget to judge whether the thing's worth claiming.
  • We Points can't inflate, and can't be so stingy no one gives. The line's thin.
  • No money changes hands. Why trust a stranger? Trust has to be built into every signal.
  • Scrolling video and browsing a grid are two rhythms. They have to share one product without fighting.

4Key Features I Designed

Short-Video Item Feed
Full-screen vertical swipe, same gesture as scrolling short video—the thing's right there, no tap needed to see it.
We Points Economy
Give, earn points; claim, spend points. One rule. Nobody loses, the wheel turns.
Dual Browse Modes
Don't know what you want? Scroll. Know what you want? Grid. Two rhythms, one product.
3-Step Posting
Film, describe, confirm. Three steps. Giving shouldn't be heavier than posting a moment.
"Want It!" Claiming
Tap "Want it"—the modal tells you the point cost and how it gets to you. Nothing hidden.

What I Learned
Borrowing a gesture everyone already knows (scrolling short video) to trigger a new behavior (giving stuff away) beats teaching from scratch. The hard part: two feelings have to land at once—the moment you give, the reward has to be instant; the moment you claim, it has to feel fair and clean. Lose either, the loop stalls.

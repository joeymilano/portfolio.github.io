1Background & Motivation

"Everyone has things they no longer need, but throwing them away feels wasteful. What if giving them away could be as fun as scrolling TikTok?"

Existing second-hand platforms like Xianyu (Idle Fish) focus on buying and selling, which introduces friction: pricing, bargaining, and trust issues. Many people have perfectly usable items they'd happily give away for free - but there's no engaging platform designed specifically for gifting.

WeNeed reimagines the experience by combining the addictive UX of short-video feeds with a zero-cost gifting model. Instead of money, the app uses We Points - a virtual currency earned by giving away items and spent to claim others. This creates a self-sustaining circular economy where generosity is rewarded.

3Design Challenges

  • Designing a video-first feed that feels as natural as TikTok while serving a fundamentally different purpose - item discovery rather than entertainment
  • Creating a virtual currency system (We Points) that feels fair and intuitive - balancing earning through giving with spending to claim, without creating exploitation loopholes
  • Building trust in a free gifting platform - users need confidence that items are genuine and the process is safe, without the monetary commitment that normally signals intent
  • Seamlessly blending two distinct UX paradigms: immersive dark video feed (TikTok-style) and structured light browse grid (e-commerce-style) within one coherent app

4Key Features I Designed

Short-Video Item Feed
Full-screen vertical video feed with swipe-to-browse. Each item is showcased through a 15-60s video with overlay info: item name, description, We point cost, and social actions (like, collect, share). Video backgrounds with gradient overlays create an immersive browsing experience.
We Points Virtual Economy
Users earn We Points by giving away items (+15 points per successful gift) and spend points to claim items (typically 10 points). New users receive starter points. The system creates a virtuous cycle: give more, get more, reducing waste while building community.
Dual Browse Modes
Home feed (dark, immersive video) for discovery and Browse tab (light, structured grid) for targeted search. Category filters (Accessories, Electronics, Books, Stationery) with search functionality allow purposeful item hunting.
3-Step Publishing Flow
Streamlined item posting: (1) Record/upload a video showcasing the item, (2) Add name, description, and delivery preference, (3) Preview and confirm with We point settings. Progress indicator and validation at each step reduce drop-off.
Want It! Claiming System
One-tap "Want It" button triggers a detail modal with item info, We point cost breakdown, and delivery options (self-pickup, campus delivery, express). Clear pricing transparency builds trust in the free gifting model.

Starting from user research on college students' second-hand item pain points, I mapped the information architecture around two core loops: the giving loop (post > match > deliver > earn points) and the getting loop (browse > claim > receive > review). The UI design evolved through multiple iterations, balancing the fun of video content with the practicality of item transactions.

What I Learned
This project taught me that the best UX borrows from familiar patterns but serves new purposes. By using TikTok's addictive vertical video format for item discovery, we lowered the learning curve to near zero while making the gifting experience genuinely enjoyable. The We Points system was the hardest design challenge - getting the economics right required thinking like both a product designer and a behavioral economist. The key insight: in a gifting economy, the reward for giving must feel more satisfying than the cost of claiming, or the system collapses.
